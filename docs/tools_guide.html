MEGA Tools Guide

Quick overview of the interactive CSV‑powered tools and how to use them.

This page is a human‑friendly map of the MEGA League tools. Each card links to an HTML report that is fully driven by the CSV exports in this repo – pick a tool, follow the “How to use” steps, and explore.

Game Planning & Matchups

Matchup Gameplan Advisor
Open tool

Compare any two teams using season‑long and recent‑form stats, with automatic strength/weakness labels and rule‑based suggestions for how to attack, protect, and defend.

How to use
  • Select your team and opponent from the dropdowns.
  • Optionally switch to the “Last 4 games” window for recent form.
  • Read the overview, matchup panels, and suggestions to shape your weekly gameplan.
Best for: weekly opponent scouting and play‑calling ideas.
Week 18 Playoff Simulator
Open tool

Interactive what‑if simulator for the final week of the season that uses team and game CSVs to recompute standings and playoff outcomes as you change results.

How to use
  • Open the simulator and locate each Week 18 matchup.
  • Toggle winners/losers and ties for the games you want to explore.
  • Watch the live updates to standings and playoff berths.
Best for: exploring clinching scenarios and tiebreakers.

Team Identity & Efficiency

Stats Dashboard Hub
Open hub

Visual landing page that connects all major analytics views built from the team‑level CSV exports.

How to use
  • Start here when you’re not sure which visualization you want.
  • Hover each card to see what metrics it covers.
  • Click through to open the detailed explorer pages.
Best for: discovering which tools are available.
Team Stats Explorer
Open tool

Scatter‑plot explorer that shows how different offensive and defensive metrics correlate with win percentage across the league.

How to use
  • Pick a metric on the X‑axis to compare against win%.
  • Hover points to see the specific team and values.
  • Use the trend line and quadrants to understand which styles actually win.
Best for: testing “what really matters to winning” theories.
Cross‑Metric Correlations
Open tool

Pre‑built correlation views that compare offense vs defense, passing vs rushing, and other metric pairs.

How to use
  • Browse the list of correlation charts down the page.
  • Open the view that matches the question you care about (e.g., pressure vs coverage).
  • Scan the R² and slopes to see which relationships are strongest.
Best for: deeper statistical research and presentation slides.

Player Usage, Trades & Roster Context

Player Usage Distribution
Open tool

Breaks down how each team allocates targets and touches across WRs, TEs, and RBs using the player‑level CSV exports.

How to use
  • Select a team from the controls at the top.
  • Study target share bars for WR/TE/RB and red‑zone usage.
  • Compare concentrated vs spread usage and how it ties to turnovers and sacks.
Best for: understanding where an offense actually flows.
Trades & Multi‑Team Players
Open tool

Highlights players who logged stats for multiple teams and shows how their production is split across each stint.

How to use
  • Filter to a team or player of interest.
  • Review yards, TDs, and efficiency for each team stint.
  • Use it to quantify how much trades changed a team’s offense.
Best for: analyzing trade impact and rental players.
Rankings Explorer
Open tool

Combines power rankings, cap data, and performance metrics to show team philosophies and balance between offense and defense.

How to use
  • Switch between views for offensive vs defensive rank, volatility, and cap efficiency.
  • Look for teams that over‑ or under‑perform their underlying metrics.
  • Use the patterns to spot buy‑low or sell‑high opportunities.
Best for: big‑picture roster and franchise strategy.

Schedule & Playoff Context

Strength of Schedule Analysis
Open tool

Visualizes completed and remaining strength of schedule, tying opponent difficulty back to win% and playoff odds.

How to use
  • Choose the SoS view you care about (completed, remaining, or total).
  • Hover points to see which teams had the toughest and easiest paths.
  • Use it alongside playoff race views to explain over‑ and under‑achievers.
Best for: context around records and playoff resumes.
All tools read directly from the CSV exports in this repo. Regenerate them with python3 scripts/run_all_stats.py before a new season or data refresh.
